## What V.O.N.N.I.E. Actually Is

V.O.N.N.I.E. is an **AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) tool** — a category of assistive technology used by people who cannot speak due to:

- Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
- ALS (Lou Gehrig's Disease)
- Stroke / Aphasia
- Cerebral Palsy
- Spinal Cord Injury / Quadriplegia
- Parkinson's Disease
- Autism (non-verbal)
- Post-surgical laryngectomy (throat cancer)
- Intubation / ICU recovery (temporary non-verbal state)

Traditional AAC devices (Tobii Dynavox, PRC-AAC, Lingraphica) cost **$4,000–$15,000** per device. V.O.N.N.I.E. runs on any smartphone, tablet, or browser. **That is the core market disruption.**

## Target Users & Industries

---

### 🏥 INDUSTRY 1 — Healthcare / Hospitals

**Who:** ICU nurses, speech-language pathologists (SLPs), occupational therapists (OTs), hospital communication teams

**The problem they have:** Intubated patients and post-surgical patients cannot speak. Hospitals use whiteboards and laminated letter boards — tools unchanged since the 1970s. Staff report that communication failure during ICU stays is one of the top drivers of patient distress, falls, and medical errors.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** A tablet mounted at the bedside gives a patient one-tap access to common needs (pain, restroom, reposition, call nurse) without requiring the patient to hold anything or perform fine motor tasks. The AI "Say Anything" mode handles requests the preset buttons don't cover.

**Revenue model:**
- **Hospital Site License:** $299/month per unit/tablet, or $2,400/year
- **Department Bundle:** 10 tablets = $1,800/month
- **Hospital-wide License:** $8,000–$25,000/year (size-based)

**How to sell:**
- Target Director of Patient Experience and Chief Nursing Officers
- Partner with medical device distributors (Hill-Rom, Stryker)
- Submit to hospital GPO (Group Purchasing Organizations) like Premier and Vizient for catalog listing
- Present at ASHA (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association) annual conference

### 🏠 INDUSTRY 2 — Home Health & Family Caregiving

**Who:** Family members caring for a loved one with ALS, TBI, stroke, Parkinson's, or dementia at home. Home health aides. Private-duty nurses.

**The problem they have:** Expensive dedicated AAC devices are out of reach for most families. Insurance coverage is inconsistent and requires a 6-12 month SLP evaluation process. Meanwhile the person who needs communication help is suffering today.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** V.O.N.N.I.E. runs on an existing iPad or Android tablet the family already owns. Setup takes 10 minutes. The caregiver configures the buttons (custom button editor) to match this specific person's life — their family members' names, their favorite shows, their real daily needs.

**Revenue model:**
- **Family Plan:** $9.99/month or $79/year per household
- **Lifetime License:** $149 one-time (strong conversion for caregivers who want permanence)
- **Free tier:** Core 12 buttons, no custom buttons, no AI chips (drives upgrades)

**How to sell:**
- ALS Association chapter outreach (they actively push free/low-cost tools to members)
- ALS TDI, Stroke Warrior Foundation, TBI Hope & Inspiration — disability nonprofit newsletters
- Facebook Groups: "ALS Caregiver Support," "TBI Survivors and Caregivers," "Quadriplegia Life"
- YouTube caregiving channels — demo video + affiliate link
- Google Ads: "AAC app for ALS," "communication app for stroke patients"

### 🏫 INDUSTRY 3 — Special Education / Schools

**Who:** Special education teachers, school speech-language pathologists, paraprofessionals supporting non-verbal students

**The problem they have:** Schools are legally required under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) to provide communication devices for students who need them. But district budgets are tight, dedicated AAC devices take months to procure, and they often break or get lost. Teachers need something they can use *today*.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** Runs on a school Chromebook or existing iPad. Teacher customizes buttons for each student's vocabulary, interests, and daily routine. The AI chips generate contextual suggestions that help students expand their communication.

**Revenue model:**
- **Classroom License:** $199/year per classroom (up to 30 devices)
- **District License:** $1,500–$8,000/year based on enrollment
- **Title I discount pricing:** 50% off for qualifying schools

**How to sell:**
- ASHA conference exhibit hall
- Council for Exceptional Children (CEC) annual conference
- Direct outreach to district special education directors
- State department of education AT (assistive technology) coordinator lists
- Partner with GoGuardian, Clever, or other EdTech platforms for school SSO distribution

### 🏡 INDUSTRY 4 — Skilled Nursing Facilities & Long-Term Care

**Who:** Nursing home directors of nursing, activities directors, speech therapists on staff

**The problem they have:** SNFs house thousands of residents with dementia, stroke, and Parkinson's who have lost verbal communication. Staff-to-resident ratios are low. When a resident can't communicate a need, it gets missed — leading to falls, pressure sores, medication errors, and distress.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** A tablet or wall-mounted display in each resident's room with their personalized button set. Family phone numbers built in for call-family feature. Staff can review the communication log to understand patterns ("she asks for repositioning every 2 hours").

**Revenue model:**
- **Per-Facility License:** $399/month (up to 50 residents) or $3,200/year
- **Enterprise Chain License:** Contact for pricing (chains like Sunrise Senior Living have 500+ facilities)
- **CMS reimbursement angle:** SNFs can potentially bill AAC device use under Medicare Part B with an SLP evaluation

**How to sell:**
- American Health Care Association (AHCA) / National Center for Assisted Living (NCAL) conferences
- State long-term care association meetings
- Target Regional Vice Presidents of Operations for large SNF chains
- Pitch to SNF purchasing managers through group purchasing organizations

---

### 🤝 INDUSTRY 5 — Rehabilitation Centers

**Who:** Physical medicine & rehabilitation (PM&R) departments, inpatient rehab facilities (IRFs), TBI rehabilitation centers

**The problem they have:** Patients in acute rehab following a stroke or TBI often have a temporary communication impairment. They need a tool for the 2-6 week rehab stay while they regain verbal function, but a $10,000 device is not warranted for a short-term need.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** Per-patient licensing means the facility pays a small fee per admission. The patient has a fully functional AAC tool for the duration of their stay. The button set is customized by the SLP at intake, saved to the device, and deleted when the patient discharges.

**Revenue model:**
- **Per-Patient-Day:** $2–$4/day per patient using the app
- **IRF Site License:** $599/month for the facility
- **Integration add-on:** $1,500 setup fee for EHR integration (Epic, Cerner) to auto-populate patient name and care team in the app

---

### 🌍 INDUSTRY 6 — International / NGO / Low-Resource Settings

**Who:** NGOs working in disability inclusion, international development orgs, global health nonprofits

**The problem they have:** In low- and middle-income countries, $10,000 AAC devices are simply not an option. Children and adults with communication disabilities have almost no tools.

**How V.O.N.N.I.E. solves it:** A $9.99/year app that runs offline on a $50 Android tablet is genuinely accessible. Multi-language support (roadmap item) makes it globally deployable.

**Revenue model:**
- **NGO/nonprofit pricing:** $2/year per user (volume)
- **Grant-funded deployment:** Apply to AT Act grants, USAID disability inclusion programs, and Gates Foundation global health grants
- **CSR partnerships:** Corporate accessibility budgets at companies like Microsoft, Google, Apple that fund disability tech
